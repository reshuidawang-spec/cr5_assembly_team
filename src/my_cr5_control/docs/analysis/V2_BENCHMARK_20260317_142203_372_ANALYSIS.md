The new `v2` scene family is no longer dominated by one uniform pattern. Different planners break on different scenes:

- `Easy_HoleCenter`
  - still contains long-tail risk for several classical planners
  - `FMT / PRMstar` hit budget on `4/10`

  - now becomes one of the hardest discriminative scenes
  - `LBTRRT` hits budget on `6/10`
  - `FMT` hits budget on `7/10`
  - `HeuristicGuided` stays below budget but median rises to `563.0 ms`
- `Hard_DeepInterior`
  - `HeuristicGuided` remains controlled
  - `FMT / BFMT / LBTRRT` still show `20%` timeout-ish behavior
- `Extreme_NarrowPassage`
  - now reflects a real retained cavity feature
  - `LBTRRT` hits budget on `6/10`
  - `FMT` hits budget on `5/10`
  - `HeuristicGuided` keeps `0/10` budget hits, median `71.0 ms`

This is the strongest paper-relevant signal in the run:

- the classical planners are not failing in the sense of “cannot solve”
- they are failing in the sense of heavy long-tail latency under constrained geometric access
- `HeuristicGuided` still provides the most robust latency control

## 5. Paper Interpretation

This run strengthens the paper storyline in two ways:

1. The `v2` benchmark is now closer to a real STL-driven constrained measurement scene, not just a bbox-level heuristic cavity demo.
2. The main value of `HeuristicGuided` is again not “best raw median in every row”, but “near-elimination of budget-limit risk under constrained geometry”.

That second point is especially important. Even after moving to the current `2-cavity` extractor:

- `HeuristicGuided` budget hit rate remains `0.0%`
- every classical baseline still shows non-trivial long-tail exposure

So the most defensible paper claim is still:

`HeuristicGuided` improves planning stability and suppresses the long-tail budget-hit behavior that appears in constrained measurement scenes.
